# L5 — GROWTH: Channel Position & 90-Day Sequence

> Layer owner: GROWTH. Contains the cross-document synthesis (§7 of the mandate). Depends on ratified L1–L4.

## Executive summary

- The website's winnable position is not "cheaper than Shopee" (a race we'd advertise into Shopee's face) but **"the freshest, most personal way to buy Dekaka, delivered today in Bandung"** — with the price advantage discovered at checkout, not shouted.
- The KPI math is unforgiving: 500 orders/month at 2.5% CVR needs ~20,000 sessions/month — traffic the brand's IG cannot produce organically in 90 days. The honest Week-12 target is **250–300 orders/month** (which also matches the L4 solo ceiling).
- Loyalty points ship at launch (already built, already promised in UX) but marketed as a whisper, not a pillar — WA relationship beats point math for Ibu RT; points exist to justify the web-vs-offline price gap.
- B2B for 90 days = landing page + WA inquiry + prepaid manual deals. Deals close on **samples, price sheet PDF, and a fast human** — not on portals.
- Soft launch (invite/IG-close-friends, Week 0–2) before public IG push (Week 3), gated on Phase-0 ops metrics, not the calendar.

## Core question

How does the website win customers without overbuilding — and what is the 90-day sequence?

## Depth analysis table

| Depth | Current state | Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **D1 Surface** | Full-featured store, empty blog, static testimonials | Overbuilt surface vs zero brand-site awareness; looks big, is new | Launch copy leans on heritage + same-day Bandung, not feature breadth |
| **D2 Operational** | Growth events (promos, IG pushes) create order spikes | A spike above the L4 ceiling degrades the differentiator (see L4 pre-mortem) | Every marketing push scheduled against capacity headroom; no promo in a week already >70% ceiling |
| **D3 Financial** | Points liability ~1–1.2% of net product revenue (L2 Rule 3); coupons capped (Rule 6) | Growth bought with discounts inverts the ladder | Acquisition promo = free item add-on (siomay sampler) not price cuts — protects ladder, samples catalog |
| **D4 Strategic** | Shopee: reach + subsidies; WA: trust + labor-heavy; offline: cheapest; GrabMart: impulse | Website cannibalizing WA orders is fine (less labor); cannibalizing offline is not | Position website as the *upgrade path* for WA/IG followers; leave Shopee for discovery |
| **D5 Existential** | 90 days of flat traffic → founder demoralization → quiet abandonment | The realistic kill scenario is not scandal but *drift* | Weekly KPI ritual with pre-agreed floors; explicit "pivot/persevere" checkpoint at Week 8 |

## Stakeholder rotation

- **Ibu RT**: converts from IG/WA when ordering is *easier than WA chat* and arrives today. Speed + map-pin checkout is the pitch.
- **Warehouse**: growth sequencing must respect packing capacity (L4 time model).
- **Bashara**: 90-day sequence must fit the 20 ops + freeze-limited dev hours; marketing time is a third budget (~5 hrs/week — the real constraint on content plans).
- **Brand owner**: wants the ladder intact and B2B credibility. The website is her B2B business card even at low B2C volume — that alone justifies V1. ⚠️ **STAKEHOLDER CONFLICT**: operator wants web volume (his margin); brand wants channel harmony. Resolution: positioning below routes *new* demand to web rather than migrating offline buyers.

## KPI tree to targets (show the math)

```text
Orders = Sessions × CVR × (1 + repeat uplift)
500/mo  = 20,000 × 2.5%          ← PRD target: needs paid traffic or virality
300/mo  = 10,000 × 2.5% × 1.2    ← realistic Week-12 with IG (~60k reach assumed) + WA migration + 3 SEO posts
Week-4 target:  ~100 orders/mo (soft launch, mostly WA/IG faithful; CVR high ~4% on warm traffic)
AOV assumption: Rp 150–180k → Week-12 gross ≈ Rp 45–55 jt/mo; operator net ≈ Rp 3–3.5 jt/mo (L2 archetypes)
Repeat rate floor: ≥25% by Week 12 (frozen food is a replenishment category; below 20% = product/trust problem, not traffic problem)
```

## Major decisions & options

### Decision 1 — Launch mode
(A) Public IG push day 1 — failure: spike into unproven ops (L4 pre-mortem). (B) **Soft launch Weeks 0–2 (recommended)**: close friends, WA broadcast to loyal customers, promo = free sampler add-on; public push Week 3 gated on Phase-0 criteria (L3) — failure: slower start; that's the design. (C) Silent launch, SEO-only — failure: 90 days of drift. **Recommendation: B.** Irreversibility 2, Confidence **High**.

### Decision 2 — Loyalty at launch
(A) Full points marketing pillar — failure: complexity Ibu RT ignores, liability grows, and rules become R2 once balances exist. (B) **Ship quietly (recommended)**: points visible in account + one checkout line "Dapat X poin", zero campaigns for 90 days — failure: none material. (C) Radical simplify: disable points UI — failure: rips out already-built UX and removes the price-gap justification vs offline. **Recommendation: B.** Irreversibility 3, Confidence **Medium** — would change if Week-8 data shows points drive zero repeat behavior.

### Decision 3 — B2B motion
(A) Portal + quotes + Net-30 (as built/planned) — failure: L2 Rule 10 conflict, L4 surface cost. (B) **Landing + WA inquiry + manual prepaid deals + one price-sheet PDF (recommended)** — failure: less "self-serve" polish; hotels don't self-serve anyway. (C) Defer B2B entirely — failure: forfeits the brand-credibility purpose of the site. **Recommendation: B.** Irreversibility 1, Confidence **High**.

### Decision 4 — Blog/SEO
(A) Full CMS workflow + weekly posts — failure: 5 marketing hrs/week can't sustain it; dead blog looks worse than no blog. (B) **Minimum viable: 3 evergreen posts at launch (recommended)**: "Cara menyimpan dimsum frozen", "Cara mengukus dimsum ala restoran", "Dimsum halal Bandung — cerita Dapur Dekaka" — failure: slow SEO compounding; fine. (C) No blog — failure: zero organic surface for the one category (halal frozen dimsum Bandung) we can actually rank for. **Recommendation: B.** Irreversibility 1, Confidence **High**.

## Positioning statement

> Untuk keluarga Bandung (lalu Jabodetabek) yang sudah kenal Dapur Dekaka dari Instagram atau WhatsApp, dapurdekaka.com adalah cara paling segar dan paling personal untuk memesan dimsum halal warisan 德卡 — sampai hari ini juga, dikemas beku dengan benar, dengan harga lebih baik dari marketplace.

**Three things we refuse to compete on:** (1) ongkir subsidies / free-shipping wars — Shopee's game; (2) national reach in V1 — cold chain earns geography (L3); (3) discount-driven acquisition below the price ladder — the ladder is the business model (L2 Rule 1).

## Pre-mortem (90 days)

October 2026: growth failed by drift, not disaster. The soft launch went fine (80 orders month 1), the public push landed mid-Ramadan-prep when couriers wobbled, Week-6 traffic plateaued at 4k sessions, and with no Week-8 checkpoint the founder kept polishing features (freeze had lapsed) instead of doing the two things the math demanded: monthly WA repeat broadcasts and harvesting testimonials into IG proof. Repeat rate — never measured — was actually 30%; the business was working, but nobody was looking at the one KPI that proved it, so it *felt* dead and effort migrated back to Shopee.

REQUIRES: ratified L1–L4 decision records; IG reach numbers (assumed 60k — verify); founder's real marketing hours/week

2. **Soft-launch audience**: WA broadcast list + IG close friends — who exactly, and how many?
3. **Week-8 pivot/persevere criteria**: suggested floors — repeat ≥20%, CVR ≥2%, complaints <3%. Ratify numbers.
4. **Sampler promo product**: which SKU is the free-item acquisition hook (cost ≤ Rp 10k)?

## Decision Record

DapurDekaka.com launches soft (Weeks 0–2, warm audience, free-sampler promo) and goes public only after Phase-0 ops criteria are met; it positions as the freshest, most personal way to buy Dekaka — refusing to compete on ongkir subsidies, national reach, or below-ladder discounts. The Week-12 target is re-baselined to 300 orders/month (PRD's 500–1000 becomes the with-helper Q4 goal); loyalty ships quietly, B2B runs as landing-plus-WhatsApp with prepaid manual deals, and the blog launches with exactly three evergreen posts. The kill list (B2B portal, Net-30, free-shipping coupons, reviews, referrals, AI tools, push, flash sales, subscriptions, >48h lanes, vouchers page) is binding for 90 days regardless of audit findings; the go-live scope contract in §7.3 is the single source of truth for launch scope.
